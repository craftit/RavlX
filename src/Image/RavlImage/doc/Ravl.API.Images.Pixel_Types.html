The pixel classes are grouped into subtopics according to the colour space used. The relevant image conversion functions are included in each group, as well as in a complete list. Subtopics also contain conversions between the subtopic colour space and colour spaces further up the list.

Note that YUV colour components are a linear combination of RGB components; hence the U and V components do contain luminance information. (E.g. doubling R, G and B components will, after conversion to YUV, double the U and V components, even though the chromaticity has not changed.) If you need a representation that separates luminance and chromaticity completely, use the HSV classes.

There is much confusion about the difference between YUV and YCbCr, and the term YUV is frequently used when YCbCr is meant:

  • The colour spaces for YUV and YCbCr are different; hence within RAVL, the difference is manifested in the matrix values used to convert between YUV (or YCbCr) and RGB. We have introduced separate pixel classes for each to help people produce the correct colour rendering of their images.
  • The YUV colour space traditionally used by the image processing community is based on the colour space used in analogue composite colour television signals, but ignoring the offset of the black level.
  • YCbCr pixels have a black offset of 16 grey levels. These classes are relevant in the context of ITU Rec. 601 digital video sources (or high-def equivalents), such as the DVS digital video acquistion and playback cards. Hence use YCbCr only if you are operating directly on these sources or files grabbed directly from them.

The YPbPr classes are intended for a simulation of the analogue composite video format.
