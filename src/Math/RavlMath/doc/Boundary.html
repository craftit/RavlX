Boundaries of regions are here represented by the line segments that form the junction between pixels of adjacent regions. If the image is interpreted as an array of pixels on the form of square tiles, the boundary follows the edges of the tiles. Thus each element CrackC of a boundary BoundaryC can be considered to be a vector of length 1 pixel, with 4 possible orientations. It follows that the coordinate system used for the CrackC position is offset by 1/2 pixel in each direction with respect to the pixel position; thus a CrackC position of [i,j] is always at the "top left-hand" corner of pixel [i,j]:

This representation as the gap between the pixels is in contrast to the representation in Edges.
